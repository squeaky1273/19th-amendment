Women Suffrage

During the early history of America, women suffered from a lack of rights and had to follow conventions on how they should behave

This was the time that women had to suffer from all of this consequences, for example, they couldn't own a property and had no legal claim to any money.

Women decided to start a campaign called the Women Suffrage Campaing, it started before the Civil War. It was a small campaign but it grew over time, many American women were resisting to the conventional ideas of women's rights.

Seneca Falls Convention

The movement kept small until 1848 that the movement started to get some national recognition

On July 19, 1848 the Seneca Falls convention was held, this was a two day convention in Wesleyan Chapel, Seneca Falls, New York. There were an estimated 300 attendees, a lot of men and women that worked in the anti-slavery movement joined the convention, and they eventually shifted to the fight to obtain equal rights for women.

Elizabeth Cady Stanton made her public speech the first day of the convention which helped people understand the main purpose of the meeting.


We are assembled to protest against a form of government, existing without the consent of the governed – to declare our right to be free as man is free, to be represented in the government which we are taxed to support, to have such disgraceful laws as give man the power to chastise and imprison his wife, to take the wages which she earns, the property which she inherits, and, in case of separation, the children of her love; laws test against such unjust laws as these that we are assembled today, and to have them, if possible, forever erased from our statute-books, deeming them as a shame and a disgrace to a Christian republic in the nineteenth century.…

Elizabeth Cady Stanton

After her speech Elizabeth read the Declaration of Sentiments, this was a document that expressed the goal of granting women the rights and freedoms that the Declaration of Independance granted to men.

Declaration of Sentiments

The Declaration of Sentiments was a document read during the Seneca Falls Convention that stated that women should receive equal rights just as men, it was based on the model of the American Declaration of Independance and it asked to be acknowledged and respected by society.

The document was signed by 62 women and 32 men
