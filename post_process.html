Life After the Amendment Passed

Under the 19th amendment, women, excluding Native American women, were given the right to vote. Despite having the right to vote, stipulations and roadblocks were put up to limit women, especially Black women and other minority backgrounds, at the ballot box.

Road blocks included:

  • Poll taxes
  • Literacy tests
  • etc.

Native Americans were not recognized as citizens until 1924 throught that passage of the Snyder Act (Indian Citizenship Act).

While native-born women were impacted by the 19th amendment, immigrant women were barred from voting until the Immigration and Nationality Act of 1952 which gave them citizenship and therefore voting rights.

Later on in the 20th century did the states who refused ratification, then formally ratify the 19th amendment. (My Mother Made Me Do It A Short History of the Nineteenth Amendment. - Dominican Libray)
